Move work forward
A lot of work starts as quick notes or rough ideas—and it might not be obvious how Copilot can help at that stage. These prompts show ways to use Copilot to shape early thinking into something you can use. The Microsoft 365 Copilot app is included with your plan, so you can try these prompts right away.
Get help clarifying your thinking
If you're working on a draft or idea that isn't fully formed, Copilot can help. With this prompt, Copilot starts with a short example, and then asks you to paste what you're working on:
"I need to give feedback to a coworker, but I'm not sure what to say or how to say it. In one or two sentences, show an example of turning a rough, general idea into text that I can work with. Then invite me to share a draft or message I'm working on. After I do that, suggest ways to make the text more clear, meaningful, and easy to follow. Do not ask questions or explain your changes."

Get more relevant responses for work
Adding a file to your prompt gives Copilot more context, which can lead to more useful responses. This example shows how to include a file using / so Copilot can work with it:
"Create a business report with findings from /file"

Get help saying it just right
Here's a popular prompt for revising what you already have. Replace [text] with the text you have so far. It might be a sentence, a paragraph, or more:
"Rewrite this to sound more professional and less verbose: [text]"
